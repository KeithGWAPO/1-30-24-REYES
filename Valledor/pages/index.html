    Rules of Baseball:

    1. The uncaught third strike, sometimes called the dropped third strike, is a rule in baseball involving the catcher.
    2. Though not technically a rule in itself, tie goes to the runner is a very popular interpretation of the out rules in baseball.
    3. The infield fly rule is a controversial rule that allows the on-field umpires to call a batter or runner out if they hit a fly ball in the infield with players on base.
    4. A balk occurs when a pitcher performs an illegal motion, usually pretending to pitch the baseball without actually performing a pitch.
    5. In baseball, there are both fair and foul balls. Fair balls are batted balls that land inside the first and third base foul lines.
    6. The “pinch” substitution rule is one that is fairly unique to the sport of baseball.
    7. Tagging up is an extremely important rule in baseball and plays a key role in how teams run the bases and record outs.
    8. The ambidextrous pitcher rule was added when pitcher Pat Venditte joined the MLB.
    9. Similar to football and basketball, Major League Baseball has a video replay review system in order to review certain types of calls.
    10. A ground rule double occurs when a ball is fairly hit and lands in the field of play but then leaves the field of play without interference from a fielder.
